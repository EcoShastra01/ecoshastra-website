About Us

Where economic thinking gets built, not just taught.

Eco Shastra is the official Economics Society of Sri Aurobindo College (Evening), University of Delhi. We exist to make economics tangible — turning theory into discussion, data into debate, and classroom concepts into real-world thinking.

Through panel discussions, workshops, competitions and collaborative projects, the society promotes academic excellence, economic awareness, leadership and creativity — giving students a platform to question, build and lead beyond the syllabus.

  • Academic excellence through discussion-driven learning
  • Leadership opportunities across eight departments
  • A creative, student-run platform for events and ideas

Departments

Eight teams, one society.

Every Eco Shastra event is built by a network of focused departments working in sync.

Content

Crafts the words — articles, scripts and research that give every event its voice.

Graphics

Designs the visual identity — posters, decks and digital assets across every platform.

Public Relations

Builds outreach and partnerships, connecting Eco Shastra with colleges and collaborators.

Marketing

Drives visibility and promotion for every event, campaign and initiative.

Human Resources

Manages people, onboarding and internal coordination across the society.

Creative

Shapes concepts, themes and creative direction for flagship events.

Social Media

Runs the society's online presence and day-to-day community engagement.

Event Management

Events

What we run, all year round.

From flagship fests to focused workshops — every event is built to sharpen economic thinking.

01

Econverse

Our flagship dialogue series — structured debates and panel discussions on pressing economic issues, from monetary policy to market disruption.

02

Ecomania

An inter-college economics fest packed with quizzes, case-study battles and simulations that test analytical thinking under pressure.

03

Workshops

Hands-on sessions on financial modelling, data analysis and research methods, led by faculty and industry practitioners.

04

Seminars

Guest lectures and expert talks that bring real-world economic perspective straight into the classroom.

05

Competitions

Essay writing, stock-market simulations and policy-pitch contests designed to sharpen practical economic skill.
